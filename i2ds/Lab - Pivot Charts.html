Pole Vaulting and Pivoting
Sometimes the following diagram is used to illustrate data pivoting:

How do you make sense of this diagram? Also, how is this related to the pole vaulter image used in the introintro?

This illustration shows how pivoting works. It starts by determining the columns (grouping) and then by default counting the number of occurrences of each item in the respective column. As seen in the illustration, there are two types of shape: triangle and circle. Then, for each shape, triangle has two items whereas circle has three items.
As to the relation between Pole Vaulting and Pivoting, honestly it is quite hard for me to establish and explain the relationship as I'm not familiar with Pole Vaulting, not even tried it in any PE classes. However, a pole vaulter jumps over the bar using the vaulter, as if it is trying to convey that any data that meets/satisfy a "rule" it will be then processed into the other side. It's like a filtering system. Imagine there are 10 students who will do pole vaulting. Some (say, 7) students successfully vaulted the bar, rendering them "passed" while the other (the remaining three) students was unable to vault it for many reasons, making them "failed" the pole vaulting grade. From this example, the column would be "passed" and "failed", and the default value for the columns is count. We would get a pivot chart that shows two vertical bars for each of the column, and that the vertical height of each column corresponds to the number of rows in each of the column.
